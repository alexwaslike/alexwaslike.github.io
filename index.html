Mission

Hello, my name is Alexandra Willis and I'm a video game developer, student, and artist. I have a strong background in programming and a developing skill in technical art that I'm using to make games. I aspire to make games that contribute to video games as an art form.

Projects

Star Wars: Galaxy of Heroes

This past summer, I worked for EA Mobile on a new collectible character game called Star Wars: Galaxy of Heroes. I wrote a tool for Unity3D to help QA and designers test the battle systems.

Wanted

During the 2015 Global Game Jam, a team of 8 people, including myself, created an interactive narrative called Wanted. As the only team member with game development experience, I did my best to help the team learn and create something they enjoyed creating.

Narrative in Animation Project (WIP)

In my ART 4020: Narrative in Animation class, my team of 4 people is creating an interactive 3D short about birds in love. I'm teaching myself Maya and technical art and blogging about it along the way.

Background

You can view my full resume here.

Games are my passion. I've spent the last four summers working for video game studios, all of which I've used C# in Unity3D. Most recently, I worked at EA Mobile in Austin on the upcoming mobile collectible character game Star Wars: Galaxy of Heroes. The previous two summers, I worked at Pixel Dash, an independent game studio local to Baton Rouge, best known for Road Redemption. The summer before I started college, I did a mentorship program at independent Austin studio Heatwave Interactive, where I first learned to write C# and use Unity3D.

I'm dedicated to helping tech and gaming minorities feel welcomed. I'm the president and co-founder of a new student organized called Women in Computer Science of 50+ members. We're hosting a game jam the weekend of November 6-8, which I'm primarily responsible for organizing, including fundraising, logistics, marketing, and all of that fun stuff. On top of that, WICS has regular tutoring sessions, professional development workshops, socials, and other events.

I'm also active in the local game development community and have spoken twice at IGDA-Baton Rouge meetings and once at a New Orleans game developer meet-up. You can watch my presentation on free-to-play mobile games from the NOLA meet-up here. I've regularly attended the IGDA-Baton Rouge meetings since Pixel Dash introduced me to them.
